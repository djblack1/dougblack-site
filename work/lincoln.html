The Challenge

Lincoln was doing something genuinely hard: repositioning a 100-year-old American luxury brand for a generation that had written it off — while keeping the loyal owners who loved it exactly as it was. New technology. New vehicles. A centennial to celebrate. A younger audience to reach. A digital presence that ranked 13th among premium automaker websites.

The work that changes a number like that doesn't often get a press release.

The Whole Site

For five years I was a consistent creative voice across Lincoln's entire digital ecosystem — the work that might not make headlines, but quietly determines whether someone feels comfortable dropping $70,000 on an SUV.

Vehicle launch microsites. Owner support content. Campaign social extensions for Instagram and TikTok. The Lincoln Legacy centennial hub: a long-form digital storytelling experience covering 100 years of brand history, from the Model L to the L100 concept. The Concepts & Future Vehicles pages, which had to make a car that doesn't exist yet feel desirable right now.

The J.D. Power ranking reflects the whole site, not just the hero pages. Which means it's evidence of what happens when everything is great, not just the stuff that gets the brief.

Lincoln Rejuvenate

The most recent brief — and the most ambitious. Lincoln Rejuvenate is a multi-sensory in-cabin wellness experience: panoramic displays showing immersive environments, synchronized scent, sound, and seat massage, all working together to create a measurable moment of calm. The science backs it up: 98% of participants preferred it over a neutral in-cabin experience, with a 33ms increase in heart-rate variability.

My job was to translate what is essentially a spa treatment that happens inside a car into language that felt premium, credible, and genuinely desirable — without tipping into either tech-spec territory or wellness-brand cliché.

Translating the Technology

Two of the harder briefs (the Lincoln Digital Experience and Lincoln Black Label) had me demystifying complicated briefs.

The Digital Experience was Lincoln's next-generation in-vehicle ecosystem — Google Built-In services, personalized driver profiles, connected services, panoramic displays. Automotive tech copy defaults to either spec sheets or vague luxury-speak. The job was to frame it as a natural extension of Lincoln's sanctuary philosophy: technology that adapts to you rather than demanding your attention. The platform pages had to earn that positioning through specific, credible language — not just the claim.

Black Label is Lincoln's ultra-premium tier — curated interior themes, hand-selected materials, concierge ownership services, exclusive dealer experiences. It needed language that felt genuinely elevated without the usual luxury-brand throat-clearing. Less trim level, more world you're being invited into.

Redeem Your Dream

Lincoln Access Rewards' flagship loyalty program. I concepted it, named it, wrote it, and brought it to execution. The brief was to reward owners in a way that felt genuinely Lincoln — not points and discounts, but aspirational experiences delivered in language that makes a sweepstakes feel like an invitation.

The Through-Line

A history page, and a sweepstakes, and an elevated new feature have almost nothing in common, except that they all had to sound unmistakably Lincoln. Specifically, a Lincoln that felt contemporary without disowning its heritage, premium without being cold, and confident without being smug.

I kept that consistency across hundreds of touchpoints, over five years, across a brand in active transformation.
