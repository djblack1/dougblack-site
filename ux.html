How I got here.

I came up as a journalist, then as a brand copywriter. Somewhere along the way, the work shifted: the copy that mattered most wasn't the ad — it was the button, the error state, the onboarding flow, the sentence that clarified everything for the user. The brands that take that seriously are the ones I want to work with.

Goldman Sachs

Financial Services  ·  Internal HNW Platform  ·  NDA
Role: Voice architecture, tone guidelines, and copy standards.

Enterprise financial products are typically written by compliance teams, not communicators — which produces language that is legally accurate and humanly opaque. The system I built let the platform maintain required precision while sounding like it was written by someone who respected the reader's intelligence.

Principles from the voice doc

Assume expertise. Never assume familiarity with this tool.

Every data point is a sentence. Make it a short one.

If compliance requires language, compliance writes it. Everything else is voice.

Confidence without formality. This audience doesn't need to be impressed — they need to be respected.

Evercore

Fintech  ·  Consumer Product  ·  NDA
Role: Brand voice, tone guidelines, and messaging framework.

Evercore — one of the most respected names in institutional finance — was building for individual investors for the first time. The voice system had to honor institutional credibility while reaching an entirely new audience. I built it from zero.

Principles from the voice doc

Institutional trust, consumer clarity. Both. Always.

Don't soften institutional language. Replace it.

The reader is smart. They're also new to this product. Those aren't the same thing.

Formality is a dial, not a setting.

Verizon Wireless

Telecom  ·  Full Redesign  ·  With AKQA
Role: Voice, tone, and language system across VerizonWireless.com and the MyVerizon app.

One of the highest-traffic consumer websites in the United States. The existing site had accumulated years of copy written by different teams with different conventions. I replaced that accumulation with a system — vocabulary, nomenclature standards, and copy patterns governing navigation, product pages, onboarding, support, account management, and transactional messaging.

The principle

The best interface copy is invisible. Users shouldn't notice the words. They should just know what to do next.

Scope: vocabulary standards  ·  nomenclature system  ·  navigation copy  ·  onboarding flows  ·  support and account copy  ·  transactional messaging  ·  MyVerizon app UX writing

American Express

Financial Services  ·  Mobile Campaign  ·  2013  ·  With Huge
Silver Award — Best Mobile Marketing Campaign, NYC Design Awards

The project sat at the intersection of content design and campaign — mobile-first experiences where the copy had to work as UX as much as marketing. The principle

Every word earns its space. On a small screen, there's no room for a second draft.
