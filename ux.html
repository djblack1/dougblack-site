Mortgage Tech · 2020–2021

Better.com

UX Writing Product Copy Brand Voice Embedded
The Context

Better.com was redefining the mortgage experience — moving one of the most anxiety-inducing financial processes in American life entirely online. The product promise: close your mortgage in days, not months, with no loan officers and no branch visits. The content challenge: make that feel safe.

The Problem

Mortgage is a category built on distrust. The language of traditional mortgage — rates, points, APR, escrow, title insurance — is deliberately opaque, and most people signing a mortgage document have only a partial understanding of what they're agreeing to. Better needed copy that simplified without condescending, that moved users forward without hiding what they needed to know.

The Approach

Nearly a year embedded across product, brand, and UX writing. The work spanned onboarding flows, loan application copy, error states, email sequences, microcopy, and the brand voice system that governed how the product sounded across every touchpoint. The consistent principle: one piece of information at a time, in the language a person would actually use.

Sample Copy
Onboarding / First Impression

"Let's figure out what you can afford. No credit check yet — just a few questions."

"Your rate is locked. That means it won't change no matter what happens to the market while your loan is processing."

"This is the part most lenders make complicated. We're going to make it a checklist."

Financial Services · NDA Protected

Goldman Sachs

Content Design Voice System Product Copy HNW Audience

Specific executions protected by NDA. Work described with client permission.

The Context

An internal digital platform serving Goldman Sachs's high-net-worth client base — an audience with sophisticated financial literacy, high expectations, and zero tolerance for language that wastes their time or underestimates them.

The Problem

Enterprise financial products are typically written by compliance teams, not communicators. The result is language that is legally accurate and humanly opaque. The brief was to build a voice and content system that maintained the required precision while sounding like it was written by someone who respected the reader's intelligence.

The Approach

Developed the voice architecture, tone guidelines, and copy standards for the application — establishing how the platform should sound across navigation, data display, alerts, onboarding, and error states. The defining principle: assume expertise, never assume familiarity with this specific interface. Write for someone who knows finance but is new to this tool.

The Outcome

A content system that gave the design and product teams a shared language for making copy decisions — reducing revision cycles and creating consistency across a platform with multiple contributing teams.

Financial Services · 2013

American Express

Mobile UX Campaign Content Design
🏆 Silver Award — Best Mobile Marketing Campaign
NYC Design Awards 2015
With Huge Inc.
The Context

A mobile marketing campaign for American Express developed at Huge, recognized with a Silver Award at the NYC Design Awards for Best Mobile Marketing Campaign. The project sat at the intersection of content design and campaign — mobile-first experiences where the copy had to work as UX as much as marketing.

The Problem

Mobile marketing for a financial brand requires a specific kind of restraint: every word has to earn its space on a small screen, earn trust without pressure, and move a user toward an action they feel good about taking. The language of financial marketing — offers, benefits, rewards — is inherently transactional. The challenge was making it feel personal.

The Approach

Copy and content design developed in close collaboration with the UX and visual design teams at Huge — treating the writing as part of the interface architecture, not a layer applied afterward. Language decisions were tested against user flow, not just brand guidelines.

Award Recognition

Fintech · Voice System

Evercore

Voice & Tone Content Strategy Consumer Product

Specific executions protected by NDA.

The Context

Evercore — one of the most respected names in institutional finance — building a consumer-facing product for the first time. A new audience, new interaction patterns, and a brand identity built entirely around institutional credibility suddenly needing to speak to individuals managing their personal finances.

The Problem

Institutional financial language is precise, formal, and calibrated for an audience of professionals. Consumer financial language needs to be approachable, clear, and confidence-building for people who may not have professional financial backgrounds. Building a voice system that honored Evercore's institutional credibility while reaching an entirely new audience required establishing new principles — not just softening existing ones.

The Approach

Developed the brand voice, tone guidelines, and messaging framework for the consumer product from the ground up. Established vocabulary standards, defined the spectrum from formal to conversational for different product contexts, and created the documentation that allowed the product and design teams to make consistent copy decisions without a writer in every room.

The Outcome

A voice and content system that gave Evercore's consumer product a distinct identity — maintaining institutional trust signals while creating the clarity and accessibility that consumer financial products require.

Telecom · Full Redesign

Verizon Wireless

Voice System Content Strategy UX Writing App Copy Site Redesign
The Context

Working with AKQA on a full redesign and rebrand of VerizonWireless.com — one of the highest-traffic consumer websites in the United States. Not a refresh. A ground-up rethinking of how one of America's largest brands communicated with its customers across every digital touchpoint.

The Problem

At the scale of Verizon's digital ecosystem, inconsistent language isn't just a brand problem — it's a product problem. When millions of users move through a site journey spanning plans, devices, support, and account management, every inconsistency in terminology, tone, and labeling creates friction, confusion, and drop-off. The existing site had accumulated years of copy written by different teams with different conventions. The task was to replace that accumulation with a system.

The Approach

As part of the AKQA team, developed and implemented a new voice, tone, and language system that extended across the full VerizonWireless.com site journey and into the MyVerizon app. This meant establishing not just how Verizon should sound, but the specific vocabulary, nomenclature standards, and copy patterns that would govern every surface — navigation, product pages, onboarding, support flows, account management, and transactional messaging. The system had to be comprehensive enough to scale across a large internal team without a writer reviewing every decision.

The Outcome

A unified voice and content system deployed across VerizonWireless.com and the MyVerizon app — ensuring language consistency across the full customer journey from acquisition through account management. The kind of work that disappears when it's done right: users stop noticing the copy and start completing their tasks.

The Principle Behind the System

"The best interface copy is invisible. Users shouldn't notice the words — they should just know what to do next."

How I Work

01
Understand the user first

Every content decision starts with who's reading it and what they're trying to do. Not what the brand wants to say — what the user needs to understand to move forward.

02
Write for the moment, not the brand

Error states, onboarding flows, and empty states are not places for brand voice — they're places for clarity. I know the difference between when to deploy personality and when to get out of the way.

03
Build systems, not just copy

Good UX writing scales. That means voice guidelines, nomenclature standards, and content patterns that let a team make good decisions without a writer in every meeting.
